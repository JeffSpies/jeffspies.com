Intro

The basics.

By education, I am a computer scientist, statistician, and psychologist. In practice, I am a research technologist and methodologist with a focus on workflow, incentives, and biases. I am currently most motivated by increasing research efficiency. That includes increasing research quality, accessibilty, and diversity.

I have a Ph.D. in Quantiative Psychology (read: behavioral statistics), and co-founded the Center for Open Science (COS), where I am the Chief Technology Officer. I'm also the co-lead of SHARE and a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Engineering and Society at the University of Virginia.

Hats

I wear a few.
01

Entrepreneur

I co-founded the Center for Open Science (COS), a non-profit tech company that has raised $26M to date. Our flagship product, the Open Science Framework (OSF), was my dissertation project.

02

Technologist

I'm the Chief Technology Officer at COS, where I am responsible for strategy to accomplish our mission, software architecture, technology strategy, product vision, and external partner/funder development (with a focus on the technical). I also manage an R&D team—COS Labs.

03

Researcher and Scientist

In practice I focused more on building tools in order to help others do their science more efficiently. I incorporate scientific methodology and testing as well as an understanding of the psychology of incentives and biases in my work—technology and product development included. When I was actively doing research, I studied topics including information visualization, time-series analysis, autism, non-verbal communication, and motor control.

04

Librarian

OK, not technically, but I co-lead the SHARE project, a partnership between COS and the Association of Research Libraries, and spend a lot of time with real librarians. I like to think that I can talk digital archival, preservation, and metadata with the best of them (I probably can't, but I like to think that). I believe the library is key to improving scholarship and should play a central role on campuses especially with regard to increasing innovation and diversity.

05

Openness Advocate

Open source, open science, open scholarship, open data, open workflow--open solves many of the challenges currently recognized in the sciences and scholarship more broadly and, more than that, makes research more efficient, of higher quality, and more accessible and diverse.

06

Educator

I currently hold a Visiting Asst. Professor position at the University of Virginia in the Dept. of Engineering and Society. I also created and direct COS's internship program. It's based upon a pedagogy I developed while teaching at Notre Dame. The goal is to acheive increased learning effiency by focusing on learning how to learn in a service-learning setting with particular attention paid to acknowledging imposter syndrome and making frustration work for rather than against the learner. Engineering can be far more diverse than it is.
